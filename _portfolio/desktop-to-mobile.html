The case:

While working on our web application's alert capability, I had to solve for a way to set, edit and view notification rules in a way suited to the mobile experience. I also had to propose content for the push notifications and emails that didn't expose any sensitive data outside this secure app, while still providing value by being clear what the notification was for.

The role:

As the interaction designer responsible for the framework of the product, I worked with our mobile development team to understand various workflows as they translated to a mobile experience. In this, I advised the team in a UX capacity, and provided workflow updates for the unique opportunities a mobile environment poses, such as scrolling and viewing data-dense information.

The solve:

The design system my product worked within was still growing in the mobile element department, which led to my proposing a new order of information for the mobile notification experience.

As opposed to data tables and lists, I suggested an editable card function with more focused actions than our desktop app, for ease of user's scrolling and scanning; that could be expanded for more information on what items triggered an alert rule, and review the rule criteria themselves. I also proposed what parts of the data could be securely surfaced as content in a potentially public alert, while still being clear as to what the alert is.
